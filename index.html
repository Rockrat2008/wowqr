What is this site and who is it for?

This site was developed to be a quick reference guide for the new or casual World of Warcraft player to show you how to build a solid, playable character that is enjoyable to run through PvE (Player vs. Environment), low level Mythic dungeons, and casual raids. If you are looking to maximize your character for PvP (Player vs. Player), high level Mythic dungeons, and high level (Heroic/Mythic) raiding then you will want to do more research on your own to maximize your character build.

NOTE: Even though this is a casual guide, in some spots where this is a distinct difference between building your charcter for Mythic dungeons vs. Raiding or DPS vs. Survivability I will list options for both so you can choose where you want to focus your build.

About us and how this site came to be

My family and I have been playing World of Warcraft for years casually, running purely PvE, Mythic dungeons up to about level 10, and LFR (Looking for Raid) and normal raid dungeons. As we would level up characters we routinely found ourselves going online looking for the same information over and over again, which eventually led to building a huge spreadsheet that we would compile the information on. While there is a great deal of useful information online about World of Warcraft from elite players & guilds, most of it is designed for higher level, or aspiring elite level players looking to get the absolute most of their character build and most of these sites you have to read through a lot of information to just get a quick answer to your question. We found these tedious, and at times, overwhelming when we were first starting out. Knowing other casual players might have the same issue(s) is how this site came to be.

About the classes and specializations for each class

I have personally leveled up at least one of each class in World of Warcraft from level 1 to level 60 (current maximum level) so I feel I have a decent base understanding of how they all play. Each class has at least 2 specializations, most of 3, and some have 4. At level 1 you start with the base "class" of your character and then when you reach level 10 you can choose your specialization.

SPECIALIZATIONS

  • DPS (Damage per Second) - This class can be melee (up front fighting) or ranged using a ranged weapon or a spellcaster
  • Healer - Pretty self explanatory. In a group you are primarily healing the others, instead of focusing on doing damage. Solo you have more self-healing ability to stay alive.
  • Tank - Up front melee fighting. In a group you are responsible for leading the charge into a fight and focus on keeping the monsters fighting you so the DPS members can do damage.

Depending on your play style and what you enjoy you will naturally gravitate to one of these specializations as your preferred style of play. I encourage you to try at least one of each though as you might surprise yourself. In the beginning I focused mostly on DPS characters, but now my two primary specializations I pull out are my tanks and healers.
